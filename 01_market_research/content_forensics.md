**Date:** January 2026

**Method:** Sitemap Analysis & Keyword Inference
**Status:** ✅ Strategic Gaps Identified

## 1. Executive Summary: "Support-Driven, Not Acquisition-Driven"
**Key Finding:** PipeOps' content strategy is defensive. They rank for **"How to use PipeOps"**, not **"Why to choose PipeOps"**.

*   **Dominant Content Type:** Documentation (`/docs/`) and specific Troubleshooting Guides ("Error 502").
*   **Missing Content:** Broad "Top of Funnel" (ToFu) comparisons usually used to acquire traffic (e.g., "Heroku vs AWS", "Best PaaS for Django").
*   **SEO Posture:** They are capturing *existing* users, not *new* searchers.

## 3. Content Strategy Breakdown (The "Recipe" Playbook)
PipeOps is heavily investing in **"Open Source Recipes"** to act as a "One-Click Deploy" for popular tools.

*   **The "Marketplace" Strategy:**
    *   Content focuses on *deploying* specific tools: **Apache Superset, n8n, HashiCorp Vault, InfluxDB**.
    *   **Insight:** They are trying to position as the "Heroku for Open Source Tools".
*   **The "Defensive" Docs:**
    *   A significant portion of docs is dedicated to specific error handling (`no-buildpack-detected`, `bills-post-server-delete`), indicating frequent product friction.
