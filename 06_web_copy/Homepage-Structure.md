# Homepage Structure & Copy

**Page Type:** Universal Homepage  
**Framework:** AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)  
**Target Audience:** All segments (with segment switcher)  
**Primary Goal:** Direct visitors to segment-specific pages OR convert directly  
**Optimal Length:** 6-8 sections (~2000 words max)

---

## Strategic Overview

### Primary JTBD (Universal)
- **Functional:** Understand what Cloudsania does in 5 seconds
- **Emotional:** Feel confident this solves my deployment problem
- **Social:** See that credible companies/developers use this

### Psychological Frameworks Applied
1. **Mere Exposure Effect** - Build familiarity through consistent brand presence
2. **Social Proof** - Show customer counts, logos, testimonials
3. **Clarity First** - Remove confusion before building desire
4. **Paradox of Choice** - Limit options, guide to segment pages

### Success Metrics
- Time to comprehension: <5 seconds
- Segment identification: <10 seconds
- Primary CTA click-through: >15%

## Psychological Framework Deep Dive

### Primary: AIDA Funnel
- **Attention:** Hero headline + social proof bar (Sections 1-2)
- **Interest:** Segment switcher + core benefits (Sections 3-4)
- **Desire:** How It Works + Differentiation + Testimonials + Pricing (Sections 5-8)

### Supporting Frameworks
1. **Social Proof** (Sections 2, 7) - Customer counts, logos, testimonials
2. **Clarity First** (Sections 1, 4, 5) - Simple language, clear benefits
3. **Anchoring** (Section 8) - DevOps hire cost vs platform cost
4. **Paradox of Choice** (Section 3) - 4 clear segments, not overwhelming
5. **Activation Energy Reduction** (Section 5) - 3 simple steps, time estimates

## Page Length & Engagement Strategy

**Total Sections:** 9  
**Estimated Word Count:** ~1,800 words  
**Estimated Scroll Depth:** 4-5 screens (desktop)  
**Average Read Time:** 3-4 minutes

**Engagement Tactics:**
1. **Progressive Disclosure:** Hero → Segments → Benefits → Proof (logical flow)
2. **Visual Breaks:** Alternate text-heavy and visual-heavy sections
3. **Multiple CTAs:** 4 primary CTA placements to capture at different stages
4. **Segment Exit Points:** Let visitors navigate to specific pages early (Section 3)
5. **Scannable Format:** Headlines, bullet points, short paragraphs

**Churn Prevention:**
- Keep hero ultra-clear (5-second comprehension)
- Offer segment navigation early (Section 3) for self-identification
- Use visuals to break text monotony
- Repeat CTA at logical decision points (not just bottom)

## Implementation Notes

### Copy Tone (Brand Strategy v2.1)
- **Voice:** Clear, direct, conversational, empowering
- **Archetype:** Guide (60%) + Liberator (40%)
- **Avoid:** Jargon, hype, vendor-speak
- **Use:** Customer language, specifics, honest claims

### Visual Hierarchy
1. **Hero headline** (largest, most prominent)
2. **Section headlines** (clear visual breaks)
3. **Body copy** (readable, scannable)
4. **CTAs** (high contrast, clear action)

### Mobile Considerations
- Stack segment cards vertically
- Collapse comparison table to accordion
- Ensure CTA buttons are thumb-friendly (min 44px height)
- Reduce hero text length on small screens

### SEO Considerations
- **Title Tag:** "Cloudsania - No-Ops Cloud Deployment to AWS, DigitalOcean, and VPS"
- **Meta Description:** "Deploy to your own cloud in minutes. Zero-config deployment. 99.9% uptime SLA. Local payment accepted. Start free trial."
- **H1:** "Deploy to your own cloud in minutes. Zero complexity. Full ownership."
- **H2s:** Section headlines (9 total)
