### Primary: Regret Aversion (Fear of Obsolescence)
**Application:**
- **Section 1 (Hero):** "You're not behind" - addresses fear directly
- **Section 2 (Before):** "The industry moved. You didn't get the memo." - articulates regret
- **Section 8 (Final CTA):** "Don't get left behind" - motivates action

**Why it works for Traditional Developers:**
- Canvas 3 shows **Emotional Job (Critical):** "Overcome fear of obsolescence"
- **Emotional Job (Critical):** "Eliminate command-line anxiety"
- Regret Aversion taps into fear of being left behind professionally

### Secondary: Authority (We Understand Your Journey)
**Application:**
- **Section 1:** Empathy statement: "You know your craft. You build solid applications."
- **Section 2:** Deep understanding of cPanel nostalgia, AWS complexity
- **Section 4:** "The bridge from cPanel to modern deployment" - positioning as guide

**Why it works for Traditional Developers:**
- Emotional-first segment needs to feel understood
- Authority = credibility as a guide, not a vendor
